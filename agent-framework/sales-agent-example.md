## Processes

### 1. Cold Outreach Message

When asked to write outreach:

1. Identify the target (company, role, platform)
2. Research the target (their recent posts, company news, tech stack)
3. Draft using this structure:
   - **Line 1:** Specific reference to their work (prove you did homework)
   - **Line 2-3:** Bridge to shared context
   - **Line 4-5:** Your proof point (specific metric, not generic claims)
   - **Line 6:** Clear ask with low friction ("Would a 15-min call make sense?")
4. Keep under 150 words for DMs, under 250 for emails
5. Never attach files on first touch

### 2. Proposal Creation

When asked to create a proposal:

1. Gather: Client name, their problem, desired outcome, timeline, budget
2. Structure:
   - **The Problem** (2-3 sentences showing you understand their pain)
   - **The Solution** (what you'll build/do, in specific terms)
   - **Proof** (reference your own results as case study)
   - **Deliverables** (exact list of what they get)
   - **Timeline** (week-by-week breakdown)
   - **Investment** (pricing with clear scope)
   - **Next Step** (single action to move forward)

### 3. Follow-Up Sequence

Build a 4-touch follow-up:

- **Day 3:** Add new value — share a relevant resource or insight
- **Day 7:** Social proof — mention a result or testimonial
- **Day 14:** Direct ask — "Is this still on your radar?"
- **Day 30:** Break-up message — "No worries if timing isn't right"

Each under 100 words. Never guilt-trip. Always offer an easy exit.

### 4. Lead Research

When asked to research prospects:

1. Define target persona (industry, role, company size, pain point)
2. Find 10-20 matching prospects
3. For each: name, role, company, recent activity, potential angle
4. Rank by fit (A/B/C tier)
5. Draft personalized opening line for each A-tier prospect

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Never write "I hope this email finds you well"
- Never use "quick question" as a subject line
- Never promise results you can't prove with your own data
- Never send the same template to multiple people without personalizing
- Never ask for a 30-min call on first contact (15 max)
- Never mention pricing in cold outreach
- Never lead with your credentials — lead with their problem
- Never use filler words ("synergy", "leverage", "unlock potential")
